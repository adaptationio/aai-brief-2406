# The Offer — "AI Foothold" (adaptation.io entry rung)

A productized, low-friction offer that lands fast **and** feeds the Solutions
lifecycle model. It is the cheapest, fastest rung on the ladder — designed to be a
"yes" in one conversation, not a procurement cycle.

> Positioning: this is the **foundation rung** of adaptation.io's lifecycle model,
> sold standalone. It earns trust + cash now, and naturally upsells into the higher
> lifecycle products later. You are not inventing a new business — you're selling the
> first step of the one you already have.

---

## Two SKUs (sell whichever the prospect is ready for)

### SKU 1 — "AI Foothold Audit" (one-off, cash this week)
**Price: $400–600 (anchor $500).**

What they get, in 5 business days:
1. A **90-minute working session** mapping their highest-leverage, lowest-risk AI use case.
2. A **delivered, working automation or assistant** for that one use case
   (built and demonstrated with Claude — e.g. an inbox triager, a proposal drafter,
   a report/brief generator, a data-cleaning workflow).
3. A **one-page "what's next" map** showing the next 2–3 rungs (the lifecycle pitch).

Why it sells: it's a fixed price, fixed scope, fixed timeline, with a **tangible
artifact** they keep. No "consulting" vagueness.

### SKU 2 — "AI Foothold Monthly" (the recurring $200 engine)
**Price: $149–300/mo (anchor $249/mo). Month-to-month, cancel anytime.**

What they get, every month:
- **1 implemented or maintained automation/assistant** (new build or improvement).
- **A monthly "AI Brief"** — a short, tailored report: what changed in AI that
  matters to *their* business + the one thing to do next. (Produced with Claude;
  this is where the Max plan pays for itself.)
- **Async access** for small tweaks (capped — e.g. 2 small requests/mo).

Why it sells: low monthly number, cancel-anytime removes risk, and the "AI Brief"
gives them a reason the value shows up *every month* (fights churn).

---

## Pricing logic

- **Anchor high, allow a step-down.** Open at $500 / $249. A single step-down to
  $400 / $199 still clears the goal and closes faster.
- **One client at the anchor covers Max + margin.** You don't need volume — you need
  one or two good fits. This keeps delivery quality high while you're learning.
- **Bundle to convert:** "Audit is $500, or $250 now + your first month of Monthly free
  if you start the retainer today." Converts one-off cash into recurring.

---

## Who to sell to (tightest ICP first)

Sell to people for whom "save a few hours a week" is obviously worth $200/mo:

1. **Solo professionals & small agencies** (consultants, bookkeepers, recruiters,
   real-estate, legal/medical admin) drowning in repetitive writing/admin.
2. **Existing adaptation.io warm contacts** — past calls, inbound that never closed,
   newsletter readers, your network. Warm > cold, always start here.
3. **Niche down to one vertical** once you see what lands — same automation resold
   10× is the path from $200/mo to real money.

Disqualify: anyone who needs a 6-week scoping doc, security review, or committee.
That's the higher lifecycle rungs, not the Foothold. Don't chase it for the $200.

---

## What makes delivery cheap (and the Max plan worth it)

Every deliverable above is a Claude-native task:
- Audit use-case mapping → Claude does the first-pass analysis.
- The automation/assistant → built, drafted, and tested with Claude.
- The monthly "AI Brief" → researched and written with Claude, lightly edited by you.

So the marginal cost of delivery is mostly your review time. **The Max plan is the
factory floor.** At one retainer it's paid for; at two it's pure leverage.
