# The $200 Plan — funding Claude Max from adaptation.io

**Goal:** Reliably cover the Claude Max plan (~$200/mo) — and have it pay for itself.

**Decision:** Don't start a new side hustle. Sell the *smallest recurring slice* of
adaptation.io's existing Solutions model, delivered with Claude, so the subscription
becomes a **production cost that earns more than it costs** — not a bill to scrape for.

This folder is a ready-to-execute kit, not a pitch deck:

| File | What it is | Use it to |
|------|------------|-----------|
| `OFFER.md` | The productized entry-rung offer + pricing | Know exactly what you're selling and for how much |
| `OUTREACH.md` | Copy-paste email / DM / warm-intro sequences | Fill ~10 blanks, send today |
| `SPRINT-7DAY.md` | Day-by-day plan to first dollar | Run it; land cash in week one |

---

## The math (why the goal is small and winnable)

The target is **$200/month**, not "a business." That's ~$6.60/day of margin.
Framed as recurring revenue, it's trivially small:

- **1 client** on a **$300/mo** micro-retainer → covers Max **+ $100 margin**.
- **2 clients** at **$149/mo** → covers Max **+ $98 margin**, and de-risks churn.
- **1 one-off** "AI Foothold" audit at **$400–600** → covers **2–3 months upfront**
  while you convert it to a retainer.

The strategy below optimizes for **cash in week one**, then **recurring** — because
the bill is recurring, the revenue must be too.

---

## Why this beats the obvious alternatives

| Tempting option | Why it loses at this scale |
|---|---|
| Dropshipping / arbitrage | $1k float is too thin to absorb variance before clearing $200/mo. |
| Build an app, hope for users | Distribution is the hard part and slow — you'd pay for 6 Max plans before one sale. |
| Generic freelancing | Cash-positive but one-off; the bill is recurring, so the income must be too. |
| **Productized micro-retainer (this)** | Ugly and unscalable — but **cash-positive in week one** and **recurring**, which is the only thing that matches a monthly bill. |

**The meta-move:** the offer is *delivered with Claude*. So every hour the Max plan
saves you in delivery is margin, and the subscription stops being a cost center the
moment the first retainer lands. Use the plan to pay for the plan.

---

## How the $1,000 float is allocated

The #1 killer of a $1,000 plan is running out of cash before revenue lands. So:

| Allocation | Amount | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| First Max month | ~$200 | Buy the runway now — don't wait for revenue to start producing |
| Distribution | ~$300 | Landing page, domain/email, a small outreach-tool or directory spend |
| Infra / tools | ~$100 | The boring stuff that makes delivery look professional |
| **Held as float** | **~$400** | Dry powder. Do not spend it. It exists so you don't stall pre-revenue. |

---

## Definition of done (what "made the money" means here)

This kit has "made the money" when **one** of these is true:

1. A signed/agreed **micro-retainer ≥ $200/mo** is in place, **or**
2. A **one-off ≥ $200** has been invoiced and paid, **or**
3. A repeatable **booked pipeline** exists: ≥3 qualified prospects who've said
   "send me the proposal," with the proposal sent.

Anything less is pipeline, not revenue. The 7-day sprint is built to reach #1 or #2.
