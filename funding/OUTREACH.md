# Outreach — copy, paste, fill the blanks, send

Rule: **warm before cold.** Work the list top-to-bottom. Most $200/mo goals are met
entirely from rows 1–2 without ever sending a cold message.

Fill the `[brackets]`. Keep it short. Send from a real human account, not a brand inbox.

---

## 1. Warm — past contacts & inbound that never closed (HIGHEST yield)

> **Subject:** quick one, [First name]

> Hey [First name] — I've packaged something small and concrete since we last spoke.
>
> It's a 5-day "AI Foothold": we pick the one repetitive task eating your week
> ([guess theirs — e.g. proposals / inbox / reporting]), and I hand you a working
> AI setup that does it, plus a one-pager on what to automate next. Flat $500, you
> keep everything.
>
> Worth a 20-min call this week to see if there's an obvious first win for you?

---

## 2. Warm — your network / referrals

> Hey [First name] — small ask. I'm taking on 2–3 clients for a fixed-scope "AI
> Foothold": I build one working automation for a repetitive task in their business
> in a week, flat fee. Do you know anyone solo/small-team who's complaining about
> drowning in [admin / writing / data entry]? Happy to send a one-liner you can forward.

---

## 3. Niche cold — one vertical, specific pain (do AFTER warm dries up)

> **Subject:** [Vertical] + the [specific task] time-sink

> Hi [First name] — I work with [vertical, e.g. independent bookkeepers] on one
> thing: killing the [specific repetitive task, e.g. monthly client report] time-sink
> with a small AI setup I build and hand over in a week.
>
> No retainer to start — a one-off, you keep it. If [that task] eats more than a
> couple hours a week, it pays for itself fast.
>
> Open to a 15-min look at your workflow? If it's not an obvious win I'll tell you.

---

## 4. The "value-first" DM (LinkedIn / communities)

Don't pitch. Lead with the artifact:

> Saw your post about [their pain]. I actually built a little AI workflow for exactly
> this for another [vertical] last week — happy to show you the 2-min version, no
> pitch. Want me to send a short Loom?

Then the Loom *is* the pitch.

---

## The call (20 min) → close

1. **5 min — find the bleeding.** "What's the most repetitive thing you or your team
   did this week?" Let them talk. Pick the juiciest one.
2. **5 min — show, don't tell.** Open Claude live, do a rough version of their task
   right there. The "oh" moment closes the deal, not your slides.
3. **5 min — scope it.** "I'll build the real version, tested, handed over, in 5 days.
   $500 flat. Or $250 now and your first month of Monthly is free if you want me to
   keep improving it." Then **stop talking.**
4. **5 min — book the build.** Calendar the handover call before you hang up. Send the
   invoice within the hour while it's warm.

---

## Follow-up (where most of the money actually is)

People don't say no, they go quiet. Two short nudges, then drop:

- **+3 days:** "Hey [First name] — still keen to grab that first AI win for you? I've
  got a build slot opening [day]." 
- **+7 days:** "Last nudge — should I hold the slot or circle back next quarter?"

A clean "is this a no for now?" closes more than a third follow-up pitch.

---

## Daily target during the sprint

- **10 personalized touches/day** (rows 1–3), tracked in a simple sheet.
- Reply to every response within the hour.
- **Goal:** 3 booked calls → 1 close. That one close clears the $200/mo bar.
